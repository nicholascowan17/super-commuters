Nothing "Super" About Super-Commuting

BY NICHOLAS COWAN

Nationwide, one in 36 commuters (approximately 4.2 million Americans) are super-commuters, traveling 90+ minutes to work each day. This practice has become increasingly common, but nowhere more pronounced than in the most expensive coastal metro areas: New York, San Francisco, Washington D.C., and Los Angeles. Residing in neighborhoods increasingly distant from unaffordable city centers, these workers often spend two hours or more on public transportation or battling traffic. In light of the COVID-19 pandemic, many of these individuals likely took solace in the rise of remote work; to what extent they return to their super-commutes in the future is largely unknown.
